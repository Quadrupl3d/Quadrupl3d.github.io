Detailed course description

Build an explosive power of solving problems in mechanical physics, you will find it challenging, sometimes frustrating, occasionally painful, and often richly rewarding. In this course of mechanical physics 90% of concepts are explained using POLAR COORDINATES from SCRATCH. Currently, this course consists of chapters - kinematics, translational dynamics and rotational dynamics along with some prerequisites required for the crystal clear understanding of this course.

MASTER THE ART OF SOLVING MECHANICS: THEORY VIA MATHEMATICAL PHYSICS
PREREQUISITES EXPLAINED: Infinitesimal elements [ 1D,2D and 3D elements{all explained using polar coordinates}] + polar coordinates + cylindrical coordinates + spherical coordinates. KINEMATICS - particle motion in 1D, 2D and 3D, additive property of angular velocities, complete theory of DOUBLE ROTATION and the application of V=WR, relative motion, standard and unique problems based on chases of kinematics, projectile motion, kinematics of particle and rigid body, kinematics of a parallelogram, kinematics of a square frame.

TRANSLATIONAL DYNAMICS: Newtons laws of motion explained through problems, conservation of strings, weaving constraints into coordinates, 10+ ATWOOD'S machines, infinite ATWOOD'S machine, infinite pulleys, wedge block constraint, calculation of COM of semi-circular ring, disc, hollow sphere, solid sphere, spherical cap, plated triangle, cylindrical segment(using triple integration), calculation of center of mass of square fractal and triangle fractal, constant ratio(solved using Leibniz theorem and definite integration) , very basic to Olympiad level theory of momentum conservation and it's applications, pulling a square, H-Shape, pulling a mass, impulse momentum theory(rotation of a free body about COM, BYU question 3,CYU question 1 + more RELEVANT questions may be updated), Spring mass system, kinetic energy in center of mass frame.

ROTATIONAL DYNAMICS: theory of complete rotational motion from Olympiad point of view as it is the toughest chapter of entire physics, derivation of angular momentum of a rigid body about stationary axis and general axis, moment of inertia calculation of semi-circular ring about com, solid cone, hollow cone, hollow sphere, solid sphere, uniform rod, hollow cylinder, solid cylinder,solid cuboid systems with cavity, calculating moment of inertia using squashing , perpendicular axis theorem, parallel axis theorem, solid cone about a slanted axis, calculation of angular momentum and torque about a point and an axis. Introduction to CTRM(combined translational and rotational motion), pure rolling: special case of CTRM, radius of curvature(basics), parametric coordinates of a cycloid, Disc rolling inside a cylindrical shell of double radius, Triple rolling:COMPLETE TUTORIAL OF DESMOS COMPLETE SOLID ANGLES THEORY + ADV. TO OLYMPIAD LEVEL QUESTIONS COVERED

ENERGY + MOMENTUM + ROTATION + TRANSLATION + CONSTRAINT = MECHANICS: (currently under updation) THIS IS WHERE EVERYTHING CLICKS TOGETHER : In this section there will be questions in which all the concepts of mechanics will be integrated together. Remaining topics collisions, conservation of angular momentum, + few questions based on equilibrium statics and work, power and energy. Unlike my electrostatics course, I've not solved complete pathfinder as it is too much time consuming as well as irrelevant for some students as well, instead in this course I've integrated some of the questions of pathfinder in the theory. So, if u follow along the curriculum you'll definitely sail through it.
As mechanics is the complete start of JEE AND OLYMPIAD physics so I've explained each and every minute detail so ANYONE CAN LEARN FROM SCRATCH. In this course you will experience the depth of "MATHEMATICAL AND COMPETETIVE PHYSICS" which will in turn bolster your concepts for the upcoming chapters of physics.
Mechanics is the BASE OF COMPLETE JEE AND OLYMPIAD PHYSICS, so I've exclusively designed the theory of mechanics by using class notes, test papers of Allen score, DC Pandey, 200PPP, 300 creative problems, classical mechanics, Berkeley physics Allen's module, Pathfinder and I've incorporated the complete theory of INTERNATIONAL OLYMPIADS from scratch. As you'll go through the lectures you'll encounter a number of problems which you've never experienced or seen in your entire physics journey so let's break each and every bone of Competitive physics by breaking down tough problems into simpler ones.
So what are u waiting for? Enroll in course and boost your technical skills of solving mechanics.
